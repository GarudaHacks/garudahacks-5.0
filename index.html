Choose Your Project Track!


NEW: Each of the following 3 tracks will have no-code alternatives, the pitch competition tracks, where participants submit a 5 minute pitch video. Check out FAQ section for more info!

Health

While Indonesia is making strides to improving its healthcare system, the infrastructure providing quality care is fragmented. Specifically, a lack of well-trained physicians often impacts those who need urgent care the most. Whether your approach is preventive or curative interventions, what you create can save and improve lives, allowing people to better themselves and others.

Track focus:

  • Educate people on healthy living practices to prevent costly medical interventions in the future?
  • Provide health accessibility to ensure people get proper health regardless of location or socioeconomic status?
  • Raise awareness, create conversations, and effective interventions on mental health?

Social Good

Social justice for all, is the fifth and final principle of Pancasila, the foundation of Indonesia‚Äôs philosophy as a state. While significant progress has been made to close the gap, it is clear that there are significant systemic challenges for certain groups within Indonesian society. We believe that your solutions can create opportunities for Indonesians, regardless of creed, ethnicity, or gender, to survive and thrive.

Track focus:

  • Create fair and humane opportunities for all?
  • Educate people on systemic bias within society and help make progress on current efforts to improve equality?
  • Create solutions for a society where all Indonesians regardless of social class, ethnicity, or gender, can thrive together?

Rural Development

Home to 270 million people and 17,000 islands, Indonesia has seen rapid economic growth in the past 50 years. While some affluent regions have flourished, others have not developed as quickly. The government‚Äôs increased efforts to improve internet connectivity grants millions of Indonesians access to reliable internet for the first time, and there are many unexplored opportunities.

Track focus:

  • Integrate Indonesians living in small cities in rural areas into the digital economy?
  • Showcase the creativity and diversity of Indonesia‚Äôs culture and put them on the world stage?
  • Raise awareness and accelerate development, both at home and abroad, to developing parts of Indonesia?

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Hackathon?

A hackathon is a programming competition in which competitors use technology to build innovative projects within 48 hours. There will be opportunities to win cool prizes, learn from our key-note speakers, and network with our sponsors.

Where will it be hosted?

Garuda Hacks 3.0 will be hosted on August 26-28 2022, completely virtually. All you need is to create a Discord account to communicate with our Garuda Hacks community.

Who can participate?

Everyone can participate! We encourage anyone who is interested in learning more about Hackathons, programming, and the tech industry to join Garuda Hacks!

Do I have to know how to code to join?

Nope! You can learn along the way or team up with experienced programmers. Skills such as designing, business planning, and etc are all going to impact the projects in various ways! Also, check our new pitch competition tracks!

Are there any rules?

Attendees must abide by the Major League Hacking (MLH) Code of Conduct. We want to respect each other and keep Garuda Hacks a safe environment for all participants. We will also release a handbook closer to the date of the event.

What is the finalist round?

The top 3 hacks for each project track (excluding pitch competition tracks) will qualify to the finalist round where each team takes another 48 hours to enhance their hacks and prepare a slide deck that they will pitch live on the Demo Day. More info included in handbook.

How do I apply? What if I don‚Äôt have a team?

You can apply as an individual or as a team (up to 4 participants max). If in any case, you are looking for team members, you can form teams with other participating hackers at the beginning of our event. Don‚Äôt worry, we will help you pair up with other hackers!

How much will Garuda Hacks cost me?

Nothing at all!

How do we submit our projects?

You must submit your project‚Äôs video demonstration through Devpost. Additionally, it is optional for you to submit your project‚Äôs live website too.

What can I make?

We welcome all software, hardware hacks that align with our tracks, as well as pitch videos for our pitch competition tracks.

What are the pitch competition tracks?

For each of the 3 project tracks, we have additional no-code tracks as alternatives where participants submit a 5-minute pitch video, much like a pitch competition. One project cannot qualify for both a pitch competition track and a regular track. More info is included in the handbook.
